Greenhouse Gases Emission Around The World

Lately, the global temparature has been increasing and it is a very serious issue for us and our next generation. There are countless factors affecting the emission of greenhouse gases of a country. Thus, this visualization will discuss some of the biggest factors driving the CO2 emission higher.

CO2 Emission Around the World

The visualization shows CO2 emission per capita, which means CO2 emission per person in a country. The value is calculated by taking the total population divide the total CO2 emission.

It is clear to see that countries with better quality of life generates more CO2 per person. Australia and United States has better quality of life for their citizens compared to other countries.

Use the slider to see the CO2 emission over the years.

Non-Renewable Fuel Power Plants In United States and Australia

A conventional power plant uses non-renewable fuels, such as coal, oil or natural gas as fuels to generate electricity. These fuels are burnt to generate heat energy and the heat energy powers the electrical generators. When the fuels are in the process of burning, it emits CO2 to the air.

A waste power plant uses a similar idea as a conventional power plant. Instead of using coal, oil or natural gas, it burns munipical waste and uses the heat to generate power for electrical generators.

On the left is a distribution map of these types of power plants in Australia and United States.

Click on the legened to filter the types of power plants distributed over each country.

Comparison between United States and Australia in Agrofood CO2 Emission

Agrofood refers to food commodity or products made from crops. It is clear that United States produces more carbon dioxide than Australia in this industry as well. This is because United States has higher population than Australia, more people means more food consumption, and more food consumption means more food processing has to be done as well as food transportation.

Hover over the area to see the total CO2 emission for each category.

Deforestation of Amazon Forest From 2004 to 2019

Deforestation has been a crucial issue since as long as from the 90s. Human conducted mass deforestation to obtain the raw material for a lot of products. Mass deforestation causes less trees to absorb the CO2 in the air.

One of the biggest forest in the world is the Amazon Rainforest. On the left is a visualization showing the annual deforested area of the Amazon Rainforest.

Hover over the point to see the exact value of deforested area in each year.
