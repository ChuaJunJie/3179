Greenhouse Gases Emission Around The World

Lately, the global temparature has been increasing and it is a very serious issue for us and our next generation. There are countless factors affecting the emission of greenhouse gases of a country. Thus, this visualization will discuss some of the biggest factors driving the CO2 emission higher.

Population of the World at Year 2020

First, let's dive into a general overview of the population of each country in the world. It is clear that United States has one of the highest population in the world, whereas if we compare Australia's population with United States, Australia has lower population count. We can use the legend to compare the colour of a country, the redder the colour becomes, the more population it has.

Click on the countries to see their total population.

CO2 Emission Per Capita Around the World

The visualization shows CO2 emission per capita, which means CO2 emission per person in a country. The value is calculated by taking the total population divide the total CO2 emission.

It is clear to see that countries with better quality of life generates more CO2 per person. Australia and United States has better quality of life for their citizens compared to other countries, hence higher CO2 emission per capita.

Use the slider to see the CO2 emission over the years.

Non-Renewable Fuel Power Plants In United States and Australia

A conventional power plant uses non-renewable fuels, such as coal, oil or gas as fuels to generate electricity. These fuels are burnt to generate heat energy and the heat energy powers the electrical generators. When the fuels are in the process of burning, it emits CO2 to the air.

A waste power plant uses a similar idea as a conventional power plant. Instead of using coal, oil or natural gas, it burns munipical waste and uses the heat to generate power for electrical generators.

On the left is a distribution map of these types of power plants in Australia and United States.

Click on the legened to filter the types of power plants distributed over each country.

Agrofood refers to food commodity or products made from crops. It is clear that United States produces more carbon dioxide than Australia in this industry.
